# SceneDNA

## Purpose

SceneDNA is the immutable structural description of a real-world scene.

It defines the physical reality of a location independently of time.

SceneDNA is the foundation of every historical reconstruction produced by the LifeOverYears platform.

It describes what exists.

It does not describe how the scene should look in a specific year.

---

# Philosophy

Every historical image generated by the platform must represent the same physical place.

Regardless of the selected Era, the identity of the location must remain unchanged.

SceneDNA preserves that identity.

Historical details may change.

The physical structure must not.

---

# What SceneDNA Contains

SceneDNA stores only information that is considered structurally stable.

Examples include:

* camera position
* camera height
* camera direction
* field of view
* road layout
* sidewalks
* curbs
* driveways
* building footprints
* building geometry
* utility poles
* permanent trees
* terrain
* elevation
* visible boundaries

These elements define the geometry of the Scene.

---

# What SceneDNA Does Not Contain

SceneDNA intentionally excludes historical information.

Examples include:

* vehicles
* people
* advertisements
* business names
* prices
* road markings
* traffic signs
* weather
* lighting
* season
* vegetation growth
* temporary buildings
* construction equipment
* decorations

These belong to the selected Era or other domain objects.

---

# Immutable Identity

SceneDNA never changes because of time.

If the physical location changes significantly, a new SceneDNA should be created.

Examples:

* building demolished
* road relocated
* intersection rebuilt
* camera position changed
* entirely different viewpoint

Minor corrections may create a new version while preserving the same identity.

# Relationship to Era

SceneDNA defines the physical world.

Era defines the historical world.

SceneDNA answers:

"What exists?"

Era answers:

"What existed here during this period?"

The two objects are independent.

Neither contains the other.

---

# Relationship to Prompt

SceneDNA is one of the primary inputs for Prompt generation.

Prompt combines:

* SceneDNA
* Era
* Knowledge
* Style
* Generation settings

SceneDNA itself never contains prompt text.

---

# Relationship to Validation

SceneDNA is the reference model used to evaluate generated images.

Validation compares the generated result against the immutable structure defined by SceneDNA.

Examples of validation failures include:

* shifted camera position
* missing building
* incorrect road geometry
* altered driveway layout
* moved utility poles
* impossible perspective

---

# Design Principles

* SceneDNA is immutable.
* SceneDNA is independent of AI providers.
* SceneDNA is independent of prompts.
* SceneDNA contains no historical knowledge.
* SceneDNA represents a single Scene.
* Multiple Eras may reference the same SceneDNA.
* Multiple Prompts may reference the same SceneDNA.
* Multiple Historical Images may originate from the same SceneDNA.

---

# Summary

SceneDNA is the permanent digital identity of a Scene.

It separates physical reality from historical interpretation.

This separation allows the same location to be reconstructed consistently across multiple historical periods while preserving its identity.
